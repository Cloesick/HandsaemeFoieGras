The Artisanal Promise

This section explores the foundations of Handsaeme Foie Gras, a family-run business established in 1990. We delve into their brand identity, built on tradition, vertical integration from farm to jar, and a direct connection with consumers. The company's story is rooted in its location, "Gans-aeme" or "goose meadow," which provides a powerful narrative of authenticity and heritage.

Deep Heritage

Founded in 1990 in Kortemark, West Flanders, the company's name and location evoke a historical connection to goose husbandry, lending authenticity to its brand.

Vertical Integration

Handsaeme controls the entire production chain: breeding animals, growing their own feed, and processing everything on-site for ultimate quality control.

Direct to Consumer

The business model centers on an on-site farm shop and pre-booked tours, building a tangible connection and fostering brand loyalty through direct experience.

The Ethical Dilemma

Handsaeme's core market differentiator is its claim of producing "foie gras without gavage" (force-feeding). This section examines the nuance behind that claim. Use the toggle below to switch between the company's official marketing position and the owner's more detailed explanation of the production method, revealing the ambiguity at the heart of their ethical proposition.

The "No Gavage" Claim

"Gavage, or the forced administration of food, is NOT applied. Animal welfare is of paramount importance." This message, promoted by retailers, positions Handsaeme as a humane alternative, appealing to consumers troubled by the cruelty of conventional methods.

The Tipping Point

This section addresses the single most critical challenge facing the company: a regional ban on its core product. This legal development fundamentally threatens Handsaeme's business model and invalidates its primary market differentiator. It forces the company to a strategic inflection point, where its future viability is at stake.

2023 Production Ban

In 2023, the production of foie gras was banned in the Flemish Region of Belgium, where Handsaeme operates. This ban applies regardless of the production method, rendering the "no gavage" distinction legally irrelevant and making their flagship product illegal to produce.
